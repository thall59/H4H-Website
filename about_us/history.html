History

“I grew up in the small town of Dublin, Georgia. I was blessed with a family where love was given to me unconditionally. I graduated high school in 2013, and moved to Kennesaw to pursue higher education. Shortly after my move, my older sister also moved to Atlanta for her career as a Dietician. Being the observant and curious people we are, we quickly noticed the multitude of people on the streets that were clearly struggling to get by. This is something that I knew existed, but was never fully exposed to in the rural town of Dublin. The exposure ignited my entrepreneurial spirit, and we began to brainstorm a real and lasting way to help people. Pulling from business models like Tom’s Shoes, we came up with Hammocks for the Homeless.

But as it goes, we became busy with our personal pursuits and Hammocks for the Homeless sat on the back burner of my mind. A year later, my path unfolded into the kitchen of Must Ministries where I had the honor of serving food to the struggling men, women, and families of Marietta. This touched my heart in a way that I haven’t been able to shake since. Hammocks for the Homeless was slowly but surely edging its way to the front burner."
